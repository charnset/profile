I am a fifth year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Southern California (USC), where I am advised by Jelena Mirkovic. My research spans across different fields of knowledge, including cybersecurity, privacy, machine learning, and human-computer interaction.

I study complex problems at the intersection of humans and cybersecurity, with a particular interest in human perceptions, behaviors, and the usability of online security and privacy. To tackle these problems, I draw on my experience as a researcher and scientist, using a combination of quantitative methods such as surveys and automated approaches like web scraping to collect data at scale. I then apply descriptive and inferential statistical analyses to gain deeper insights, and in some cases, develop predictive models to uncover patterns or relationships that can be applied to larger-scale measurement. Through this process, I produce insighful findings of security and privacy solutions that are not only more usable but also provide stronger protection and better experiences for users.
